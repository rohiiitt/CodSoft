Tribute to Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist who is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.

Biography

Nikola Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, Croatia. He was known for his revolutionary developments in the field of electricity and magnetism. His work laid the foundation for modern electrical engineering, and he is often considered one of the greatest inventors of all time.

He moved to the United States in 1884 and worked with Thomas Edison before striking out on his own. Tesla's inventions and ideas were far ahead of his time, and many of them are still used today.

Major Achievements

  • Development of AC electricity
  • Invention of the Tesla coil
  • Contributions to radio technology
  • Advancements in wireless communication
